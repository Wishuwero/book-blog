Between Two Fires Blog #3

By Omar Andre

What's happened so far

  • First we were introduced to the angels. There are the "fallen" ones and the ones that still stand with god. The fallen ones are attacking earth to try to ragebait god into waking up, but if he doesn't they'll attack heaven because that would mean he's too asleep to fight back.
  • Then we meet a group of traveling thiefs. They come across a girl, two of them want to hurt her, one of them is neutral, and Thomas protects her. Thomas kills two of his companions, but leaves Jacquot alive.
  • We learn the girl approaches the group because an angel told her to.
  • They embark into another small town nearby
  • We learn that comets in the sky signal plagues nearby <-- Blog #1
  • They go to a monastery, everyone is dead
  • They go to a nearby town and meet a priest, which gives them wine
  • The priest asks them to slay a nearby beast in the lake for Thomas to "become a knight again".
  • They recruit side characters, then they slay the strange beast, with all the other side characters dying.
  • They head to paris because the girl wants to
  • They encounter a fortress, reminiscent of past times, no plague, they have a colosseum, bunch of food and music, etc, etc. The girl doesn't go in, Thomas and priest do, against the girl's wishes.
  • Things are weird. Thomas signs up to fight in the arena, king is cruel towards people. They know Thomas from before the plague and make fun of his knight origins.
  • Thomas blacks out and wakes up about to fight
  • Thomas fights but it turns out they were all demon people or something
  • Thomas is about to loose but the sun comes up and he wakes up from this "dream" <-- Blog #2
  • They reach Paris
  • Paris still has people and commerce, but it's not a "strong" city.
  • They try to find somewhere where they can sleep
  • They get scammed into buying an overpriced key that supposedly accessed sleeping quarters
  • The "quarters" were already opened, but it had rotting corpses inside, they couldn't sleep there
  • Thomas tried to find the scammers again, but he ran into a group of innocents and almost fought with them.
  • After Thomas leaves them alone, the girl sees an angel, which asks her to sing.
  • Because of the singing they find another family, they invite them inside and stuff.
  • We learn that during the night some "stone people" knock on doors and try to maul people.
  • We learn Thomas' backstory. His lord was defeated and he was dishonorably discharged by the next one in order to steal Thomas' land
  • They go to the market the next day and find a mysterious man with a bunch of holy objects.
  • A mob attacks this man and accuses him of being a Jew, then they imprison him.
  • The girl runs away and frees the man, Thomas goes with her.
  • Back at the house they open the door and fight the "stone people"
  • Their hosts die <-- Blog #3

Thoughts

The key

I didn't really like the interaction with the scammers. It felt like kind of a forced moment in order for the group to get the key, which has some kind of symbolic or magic significance. The scammers were too obvious with their scamming, they offered the overpriced key without any guarantees, they said that the place belonged to them, even though they didn't have any proof, and they literally mentioned that the previous renters just died. Even with all of this, Thomas, the big impatient untrusting guy, who could just kick down the door of the apartment (as he demonstrated moments later with the innocent family) went along with everything the scammers said and couldn't thing of staying there while the priest checked the place, or kicking down the door, or have them guide the group there, or literally anything else. It's not a big complaint, and it's the first scene that I feel was forced for plot reasons, but I thought it was worth mentioning.

The key also seems to be some kind of magic thing. It is described as too small to work, but it still unlocks things. I don't know what to make of these magic trinkets, because they were in he hands of very clearly scammers, but it was still a good thing that the group go the key. As of right now, because later we might find out the scammers were something else, I'm just going to assume that the key is just furthering the theme of destiny about the girl.
