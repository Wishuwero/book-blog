Between Two Fires Blog #2

By Omar Andre

What's happened so far

  • First we were introduced to the angels. There are the "fallen" ones and the ones that still stand with god. The fallen ones are attacking earth to try to ragebait god into waking up, but if he doesn't they'll attack heaven because that would mean he's too asleep to fight back.
  • Then we meet a group of traveling thiefs. They come across a girl, two of them want to hurt her, one of them is neutral, and Thomas protects her. Thomas kills two of his companions, but leaves Jacquot alive.
  • We learn the girl approaches the group because an angel told her to.
  • They embark into another small town nearby
  • We learn that comets in the sky signal plagues nearby <-- Blog #1
  • They go to a monastery, everyone is dead
  • They go to a nearby town and meet a priest, which gives them wine
  • The priest asks them to slay a nearby beast in the lake for Thomas to "become a knight again".
  • They recruit side characters, then they slay the strange beast, with all the other side characters dying.
  • They head to paris because the girl wants to
  • They encounter a fortress, reminiscent of past times, no plague, they have a colosseum, bunch of food and music, etc, etc. The girl doesn't go in, Thomas and priest do, against the girl's wishes.
  • Things are weird. Thomas signs up to fight in the arena, king is cruel towards people. They know Thomas from before the plague and make fun of his knight origins.
  • Thomas blacks out and wakes up about to fight
  • Thomas fights but it turns out they were all demon people or something
  • Thomas is about to loose but the sun comes up and he wakes up from this "dream" <-- Blog #2

Quotes

  1. Thomas is about to leave the girl alone, but he comes across an item: "The item that had bothered him so was a crude painted mask with horns on it. The kind a country priest would wear to play the devil in a mystery play."

  2. "He only just managed not to cry. He did this by angering himself at God for making him suffer and pay for sins he had been backed into. God ringed you round with hounds and cornered you, then speared you with your back against a tree. When Thomas spoke, he turned down the corners of his mouth, and his words came out as a quiet growl." After the priest told him if he killed the creature he'd "become a knight again".

Thoughts

I quite like this new character, I think it's because in general a priest in this world is an interesting concept. Why pray when you look around and see that god isn't there? The priests' belief hasn't been explored too much as of right now. His main conflict and theme explored right now is his relationship to wine. His introduction is him drinking wine with Thomas. At first I thought he was kind of a moral anchor, a "perfect" priest that would tell Thomas how to get back on God's good side, but it became really clear that wasn't the case during the big castle chapter. The girl advices them both to not go inside the castle. Of course, Thomas doesn't listen at all and goes in immediately, but the priest, trying to follow his priestly duties, is torn between staying with the girl and going ahead with Thomas. In the end, the girl tells him she'll be fine, to go in with Thomas. It's still unclear to me why she said that. The girl is some kind of saint, but right now she's treated as a prophet, she has cryptic messages and she herself couldn't explain why she wants to do certain things, but we can assume that whatever she says is "correct". This is a bit strange, because when he goes in, bad things happen. He too blacks out from drinking too much wine, and of course Thomas gets roughhoused by the demon people right before "waking up". My main theory on why the girl advises him to go in is that this would help him finally see wine as a problem and get the resolve to let go of it. I honestly don't remember who he was talking to while this happened, but at some point the priest was praying with someone else, and he prayed for wine, the other person told him that he hopes "god fills his heart so much that he doesn't need wine" or something, and the priest dismisses him, he still wants wine, and maybe his experiences at the castle are part of his character development to make him get past this character flaw of his.

I felt like the book kind of slowed down a little bit thematically. The castle focused a little more on character development, but even there it also felt slower. That being said, the fight scenes are still enjoyable, the dialogue during the dinner scene was really well written and overall I'm still enjoying the novel. 4/5
