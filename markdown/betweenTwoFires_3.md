# Thoughts

## The key
I didn't really like the interaction with the scammers. It felt like kind of a forced moment in order for the group to get the key, which has some kind of symbolic or magic significance. The scammers were too obvious with their scamming, they offered the overpriced key without *any* guarantees, they said that the place belonged to them, even though they didn't have any proof, and they literally mentioned that the previous renters just died. Even with all of this, Thomas, the big impatient untrusting guy, who could just kick down the door of the apartment (as he demonstrated moments later with the innocent family) went along with everything the scammers said and couldn't thing of staying there while the priest checked the place, or kicking down the door, or have them guide the group there, or literally anything else. It's not a big complaint, and it's the first scene that I feel was forced for plot reasons, but I thought it was worth mentioning. 

The key also seems to be some kind of magic thing. It is described as too small to work, but it still unlocks things. I don't know what to make of these magic trinkets, because they were in he hands of very clearly scammers, but it was still a good thing that the group go the key. As of right now, because later we might find out the scammers were something else, I'm just going to assume that the key is just furthering the theme of destiny about the girl.
