# Thoughts

I quite like this new character, I think it's because in general a priest in this world is an interesting concept. Why pray when you look around and see that god isn't there? The priests' belief hasn't been explored too much as of right now. His main conflict and theme explored right now is his relationship to wine. His introduction is him drinking wine with Thomas. At first I thought he was kind of a moral anchor, a "perfect" priest that would tell Thomas how to get back on God's good side, but it became really clear that wasn't the case during the big castle chapter.
The girl advices them both to not go inside the castle. Of course, Thomas doesn't listen at all and goes in immediately, but the priest, trying to follow his priestly duties, is torn between staying with the girl and going ahead with Thomas. In the end, the girl tells him she'll be fine, to go in with Thomas. It's still unclear to me why she said that. The girl is some kind of saint, but right now she's treated as a prophet, she has cryptic messages and she herself couldn't explain why she wants to do certain things, but we can assume that whatever she says is "correct". This is a bit strange, because when he goes in, bad things happen. He too blacks out from drinking too much wine, and of course Thomas gets roughhoused by the demon people right before "waking up". My main theory on why the girl advises him to go in is that this would help him finally see wine as a problem and get the resolve to let go of it. 
I honestly don't remember who he was talking to while this happened, but at some point the priest was praying with someone else, and he prayed for wine, the other person told him that he hopes "god fills his heart so much that he doesn't need wine" or something, and the priest dismisses him, he still wants wine, and maybe his experiences at the castle are part of his character development to make him get past this character flaw of his.

I felt like the book kind of slowed down a little bit thematically. The castle focused a little more on character development, but even there it also felt slower. That being said, the fight scenes are still enjoyable, the dialogue during the dinner scene was really well written and overall I'm still enjoying the novel. 4/5
